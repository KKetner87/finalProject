Running Alone Can Be Risky for Women


Most female runners will agree that they know “what to do to stay safe.” You are aware of your surroundings, trust your instincts, and stay within a shouting distance of other people. However, if you are ever put in an unfortunate position facing someone who is intent on harming you, these standard safety tips may not be enough to make a difference.


Midrun Harassment Isn’t Rare


In October 2016, Runner’s World published a report that discussed the harassment that many women runners endure. The survery collected responses from 4,670 runners (2,533 women and 2,137 men) show that 43 percent of women at least sometimes experience harassment on the run, compared to just 4 percent of men.


Don't Be a Victim!


You can protect yourself each and every time you're running solo with the safety app Protego. Protego allows you to set up a network of trusted contacts, who can track your route and make sure you get home safe.
